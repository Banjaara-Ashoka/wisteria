Ashoka Film Festival





What is Ashoka Film Festival?

A novel venture by a community of cinephiles, for fellow lovers of film.

Ashoka Film Festival is an organised platform for young Indian filmmaking enthusiasts to launch their films and showcase their diverse talents related to this craft, and engage with accomplished professionals in the field in meaningful discussions about their films and experiences. In the coming years, we hope to make it a talking point for filmmakers throughout the country, invite the most respected dignitaries for enlightening conversations, premier several new films, and give fresh talent an opportunity to be recognized.

Here is what you can look forward to seeing at the maiden AFF.



Screening Sessions with Indian Filmmakers

The prestige of a film festival is determined by the films it showcases. At Ashoka Film Festival, you will attend several screenings of acclaimed short films by indie Indian creators which cannot be found outside of festival circuits.

Most of our screenings would be followed by interaction sessions with the filmmakers involved.What were the challenges faced in production? Were the authorities co-operative during shoots? Where does one find the inspiration for an award-winning short? For those intrigued by the processes occurring behind the camera, here’s the chance to engage meaningfully with the ones who command it.



Talks and Workshops with Field professionals

With practical arts such as filmmaking, learning constitutes of listening and doing, and who better to teach than those who have been at it for years? Talk sessions and workshops with accomplished professionals will equip you with hands-on knowledge about the technicalities of this nuanced art form.



Panel discussions

AFF will feature panel discussions with notable filmmakers and dignitaries around important and contemporary subjects relevant to today’s cinema and audiences. These panel discussions will also feature Q&A sessions with those in attendance to clarify doubts regarding the filmmaking process, as it is shaped in India. What is the market like? Where do you get funding? What do film festivals look for? These are the topics that you would want to hear from young filmmakers on.



Short Film competition

As part of the Formal Events of Banjaara, AFF will be organizing a Short Film Competition based on student submissions. The shortlisted films would be screened at AFF and judged by respected dignitaries, who will also provide constructive feedback for the films you submit. These films will be judged in technical proficiency and conceptual depth through criteria elucidated upon in the Formal Events section of the invite.
