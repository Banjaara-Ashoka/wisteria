Brief

Jugaad Innovation is the embodiment of the make-it-happen-somehow attitude that drives up-and-coming entrepreneurs around the world. In this event teams will be given situations or problems that are routinely encountered by Indians living in villages and will then have to create a product to help solve this problem. The materials used, understandably, should be easily available and affordable by the villagers. After undergoing a two-round elimination, the winning team will secure a prize worth Rs. 10,000. Teams will have to present and explain their product in a video that will be submitted in the preliminary round. Teams that are selected will present their products and have a question and answer session with the judges.

Rules

  • 2-4 members per team.
  • Cross-college participation is allowed as long as all team members are currently college students.
  • There will be online registration for all teams.
  • The jugaad innovation must be a working model; just research without a prototype is not acceptable.
  • The first preliminary round will be online.
  • Finalists in the second round will get 5 minute to present their product followed by a 10-minute long Q/A session.
  • The equipment used to make the product should be easily available and affordable.
  • The items used can be used regularly or waste items. There are no limits or restrictions to items that can be used.

Submission Guidelines

  • The preliminary round will require teams to submit a video that explains their product and how it tackles the problem assigned to them. This video must be 5-10 minutes long and must feature all the team members.
  • The submission of this video will be via a Google Drive link that we shall share with every team.
  • The video must be submitted as a .mp4 or .avi file only.
  • The last date for submission is 25th January. Extended to 29th January.
  • Finalists who qualify from the preliminary round will be asked to present their product to a panel of judges.
  • The prototype must be transportable.
  • It is important that the jugaad innovation is a working model or the prototype. Internet research without a product will not do.
  • A minimum of one prototype must be submitted to the judges before the presentation begins.
  • The presentation must include a list of the items used to make the product, along with the average cost of production for one prototype.

Judging Criteria

  • Affordability
  • Ability to Scale the Product
  • Availability of the Items in the Village
  • Creativity
  • Convenience of Use

Prizes and Fees

  • 1st Position - Prizes worth Rs.10,000
  • 2nd Position - Prizes worth Rs.5,000
  • Individual Registration Fee - Rs.200
