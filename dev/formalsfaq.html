What is the procedure for the qualifying preliminary round?

The prelims require you to submit a one-pager and a Business Model Canvas (BMC). These will be judged based on viability, innovativeness, and usage of BMC. The top 15 teams make it to the final pitch. The subject line of the email should be ‘Prelims - PitchFork’. The body of the email must mention team code (generated through registration), the name of the team, participants, and respective institutions. Prelims must be submitted by 13th February, 2018.


What is a Business Model Canvas?

A Business Model Canvas, or BMC, is a one page document which maps the overall idea. It covers all the details in brief so that one understands the business quickly. For more information, look here.


What is a One-Pager?

A one pager is a slightly elaborate description of the business idea. It covers what your idea is, what the value proposition is, how it is relevant, etc. For information on how to write a one-pager, look here.


What is the format of the finals?

All shortlisted teams will be sent a pitch deck and a cost analysis sheet. Based on the template, teams have to present for 7 minutes in front of the investors during which time they must clearly state the amount they wish to raise and the equity they wish to give up. Post the pitch, they have 5 minutes to negotiate, with the investors, the investment amount and answer any questions they have.


Do I have to come up with a valuation and ask for my business?

Yes, you do. The final pitch will also be judged on how well the team knows the finances of the business and how rational they are in terms of raising funds.
